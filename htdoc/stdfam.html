T3. Standard Parameterized Species

  • Species that do not require imports
  • Species that require imports

    • Species that do not require imports

    A parameterized species is also called a family. The following families are available to all packages.
    [T], List(T)
    Species [T] contains lists whose members have species T. For example, [true,false,true] has species [Boolean]. Species [T] can also be written List(T).

    Optional(T)
    Species Optional(T) holds items that either are an item of species T or are the special item noValue.

    Outfile(T)
    Species Outfile(T) contains files that hold members of species T and that are opened for output. They are used for writing into files. The most commonly used form is Outfile(Char).

    <:T:>, Box(T)
    Species <:T:> holds boxes that have a content of species T. Species <:T:> can also be written Box(T).

    Species that require imports

    The following are available by importing library packages.
    DenseSet(T)
    Species DenseSet(T) holds sets that are stored as bit vectors. Species T must be Natural or an enumerated species. Import collect/denseset.ast.

    SparseSet(T)
    Species SparseSet(T) holds sets that are stored as binary search trees. Import collect/sparsset.ast.

    LinSet(T)
    Species LinSet(T) holds sets that are stored as lists of their members. Import collect/linset.ast.

    SparseTable(K,V)
    Species SparseTable(K,V) holds tables that have keys of species K and associated values of species V. They are represented as binary search trees. Import collect/sparstbl.ast.

    LinTable(K,V)
    Species LinTable(K,V) holds tables that have keys of species K and associated values of species V. They are represented as lists. Import collect/lintbl.ast.

    FunArr(T)
    Species FunArr(T) is a kind of functional array. It is similar in spirit to [T], but it uses a representation that makes random access efficient. This differs from arrays (lists of boxes) in that a functional array is a value, and cannot be changed. You can create a new functional array from an old one by modifying one of its members.
